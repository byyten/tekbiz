Problems, problems, problems

Computers are sophisticated, complex machines and are generally very reliable. Operating systems do alot to remove complexity simplifying down to a few choices in mostly logical and intuitive ways, but inevitably things can and will go wrong. Some errors can be as simple to fix as restarting the device. However a good many problems come about through having made a choice or sequence of choices (changes to a configuration) that may have solved one problem but has colateral and quite unintended consequences for something else or it could be a permanent or intermitent hardware fault or it could be a multitude of other possibilities too We can help here too but we also need your help as well. It's very helpful to know any error messages, a photo of the screen at point of failure/error is incredibly useful as well, and a clear recollection of what was being used/done at the time. Any details about what appeared on screen and the sequence of events can prove useful - all can reduce service costs and time

heart_broken

Break-fix, Second life and disposal

Electronic devices and components fail or get broken, are superceeded by new technology, become surplus to requirements or unfit for purpose and should be disposed of or replaced. They should not be disposed of in normal rubbish because they contain toxins and they contain many valuable materials in small quantities, so they need recycling and not in typical household recycling facilities. Not many facilities exist for the disposal of electronics but sometimes equipment can be re-purposed, gifted and used by less affluent or put to use in less demanding functions. We'll advise on options and help where possible. We offer a limited break-fix service, typically component replacement or upgrades like power supplies, hard drives and memory. We can also recover data in a few circumstances although this can quickly become a highly complex task best dealt with by specialist services.

Motivation

Technophiles know quite a lot about tech and it's great to share that joy

Press F1 for Help wants to help people with impartial advice, knowledge and assistance when purchasing or using computers, peripherals and electronics.

Computers are hugely capable and sophisticated devices and using one safely and effectively in the current day requires a reasonably broad level of knowledge and a good deal of common sense.

There are so many devices to choose from, and all are built, produced or optimised for different purposes so making informed choices in the marketplace can be daunting. No-one wants to throw money away and everyone wants value for money. In Information Technology you get what you pay for, but it may not always be the best or even a good choice for you

When looking to make purchases, Press F1 for Help can explain the pros and cons in frank, impartial and plain language, de-mystifying complex topics, guiding you through the process of selection and purchase, eliminating confusion or frustration and putting you back in control of what’s best for you and what you want to do.

And it doesn’t always end at hardware purchases and guarantees. There’s email, photos, data transfers, printers, inks, licenses, subscriptions, fibre or internet providers, phone usage, virus, scam avoidance, privacy and protection issues.

Sadly, there’s more and more bad actors out there, so staying safe is more and more important and there’s many that just want your money whether it’s good for you or not - Caveat Emptor has never been more relevant, or in plain language - Let the Buyer beware.

Press F1 for Help can de-mystify and reduce the complexity to simple straight forward ‘maxims’ and procedures. Advice and assistance is just a phone/video/internet call, email or in-person visit away if and when you may need that.
